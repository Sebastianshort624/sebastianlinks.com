🚨 IF THERE IS A FIRE — DO THESE IN ORDER
1️⃣
FEEL THE DOOR before opening — if hot, use window exit instead
2️⃣
STAY LOW — crawl under smoke, smoke rises
3️⃣
GET OUT FAST — do not stop to grab belongings
4️⃣
CLOSE DOORS BEHIND YOU — slows fire spread by up to 3 minutes
5️⃣
MEET AT THE STAR ⭐ — neighbor's driveway across the street
6️⃣
CALL 911 — from outside, never go back in
🛏️ Each Bedroom — Know Your Exit
Master Bedroom — primary: hallway to front door · backup: left side window
Bedroom 2 — primary: hallway to front door · backup: right side window
Bedroom 3 — primary: hallway · backup: back-left window
Bedroom 4 — primary: hallway · backup: back-right window
⭐ Meeting Spot: Designate a specific spot outside — a neighbor's mailbox, a tree, a driveway marker. Everyone meets there. Do not leave to look for someone who might still be inside — tell the firefighters.
🏃
Practice this drill every 6 months — once at night when everyone is asleep. That's the most important one.
📋 Before the Drill
Tell everyone there will be a drill (except nighttime surprise drill)
Confirm everyone knows their primary and backup exit
Confirm everyone knows the meeting spot
Make sure all windows can be opened from the inside
🔔 During the Drill
Sound the smoke alarm manually
Everyone stops what they're doing immediately
Feel the bedroom door before opening
Crawl low to the exit
Everyone meets at the designated spot
Do a headcount — everyone accounted for?
Time it — goal is under 2 minutes from alarm to meeting spot

🔋 Smoke Alarms Check monthly
Alarm in every bedroom
Alarm outside each sleeping area
Alarm on every level of the home
Test all alarms — press test button monthly
Replace batteries every year (or use 10-year sealed battery alarms)
Replace alarm units every 10 years
🧯 Fire Extinguisher
ABC extinguisher in the kitchen
Everyone knows where it is
Everyone knows how to use PASS: Pull · Aim · Squeeze · Sweep
Inspection tag is current
🍳 Kitchen Safety
Never leave stovetop cooking unattended
Keep flammables away from stove (towels, paper)
Clean grease from stovetop regularly
Toaster, microwave, and appliances are unplugged when not in use
⚡ Electrical
No overloaded outlets or power strips
No damaged or frayed cords
Space heaters kept 3 feet from anything flammable
Dryer lint trap cleaned after every use
