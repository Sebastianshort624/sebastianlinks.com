National Install Contractor Portal

Development Proposal

Project Timeline: 3–4 Weeks

The National Install Contractor Portal can be completed and operational within approximately 3 to 4 weeks.

The total investment for the project is $16,000, with a $3,500 deposit to begin development and the remaining balance paid through 5 monthly payments of $3,200 with no interest.

This allows the platform to be built and deployed quickly while keeping the financial commitment manageable and predictable.

Project Objective

The objective is to deliver a complete contractor management platform that allows National Install to manage contractors, distribute jobs, track installations, monitor project status, manage payments, and create standardized operating procedures throughout the organization.

What Will Be Included

  1. Contractor registration and login.
  2. Contractor approval and verification process.
  3. Territory-based job distribution.
  4. Job acceptance and decline functionality.
  5. Contractor notifications.
  6. Administrative dashboard.
  7. Project creation and assignment tools.
  8. Installation status tracking.
  9. Photo uploads and completion documentation.
  10. Payment tracking.
  11. Customer information release controls.
  12. Contractor performance tracking.
  13. Operational workflow documentation.

Development Timeline

Week 1

  2. Contractor portal development.
  3. User account and permission structure.
  4. Territory setup and contractor approval process.

Week 4

  1. Testing and refinements.
  2. Operations documentation.
  3. Deployment.
  4. Launch and onboarding.

Additional Deliverable

As the platform is built, the workflows, procedures, and operational standards that drive the business will be documented simultaneously.

This creates two valuable assets:

  1. A contractor management platform that supports day-to-day operations.
  2. The foundation of a National Install Operations Manual documenting how projects move through the company, how contractors are managed, and how future team members can be trained.

The goal is not simply to build software. The goal is to build a repeatable business system that can be duplicated across multiple territories while maintaining consistent execution, accountability, and quality standards.

By the end of the project, National Install will have both a functioning platform and the operational blueprint needed to support long-term growth.
