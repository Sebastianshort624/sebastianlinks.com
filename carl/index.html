✅ Do These
Eat small meals (5–6 small meals instead of 3 large ones)
Keep fat low — under 10–15g of fat per meal initially

Fried foods
Bacon, sausage
Pizza
Heavy cream
Butter-heavy dishes
Fast food
Alcohol (until cleared by surgeon)
Very spicy foods if they trigger symptoms
💡 This plan is intentionally bland and low in fat because the body is adapting to digesting fats without a gallbladder. Most people can begin expanding their diet gradually after about 2–4 weeks, depending on symptoms and their surgeon's guidance. Always follow your surgeon's discharge instructions first.

🚨 Call the Surgeon Immediately If Carl Has:
🌡️
Fever over 101°F (38.3°C)
⚠️
Increasing abdominal pain rather than gradual improvement
🤢
Persistent vomiting
👁️
Yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice)
🩹
Redness, swelling, pus, or worsening pain at the incision sites
💧
Inability to keep fluids down
🚽
Severe diarrhea lasting more than a couple of days
⚕️ This plan does not replace discharge instructions from the surgeon. Always follow the surgical team's guidance first. When in doubt, call.
🚫 Avoid ALL of these during the first 2 weeks
❌ Do NOT Eat These
Fried foods (anything fried)
Bacon or sausage
Pizza
Heavy cream or cream sauces
Butter-heavy dishes
Fast food (any of it)
Alcohol — until surgeon clears it
Very spicy foods if they cause discomfort
Large meals — stick to small portions
The body is adjusting to digesting fats without a gallbladder. High-fat and greasy foods can cause cramping, diarrhea, or nausea during recovery.
