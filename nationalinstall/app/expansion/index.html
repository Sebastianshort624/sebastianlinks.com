The Pitch

01What Happens Without a Real Platform

Right now, every lead, every photo, every quote, and every signed deposit either lives in someone's head, a text thread, or a spreadsheet that one person controls. That works at 5 deals a month. It does not work at 60 roofs a day across 4 states.

Without a platform, every new market means re-training people on a different process, every revision means someone digging through old texts to find the original quote, and every dispatch handoff is a phone call that may or may not get logged. That's not a tech problem — it's a business that can't scale past the founder's personal bandwidth.

02What the Platform Actually Does

The National Install App is the reason a secretary in Tennessee and a secretary in Georgia run the exact same process, every time, without a training manual in their hand. It's the reason a rep can pull up Eagle Eye on a Zoom call and show a homeowner their own roof, finished, before they've signed anything. It's the reason Dispatch doesn't have to guess which contractor gets which job — the system already knows.

This isn't software for software's sake. It's the difference between a business you can run from a phone and one that requires you physically present in every market to keep quality consistent.

03Why the Investment Structure Makes Sense

The build is a one-time $16,000 setup fee — not a guess, not a placeholder, a real number for a real build: client file system, photo capture, Map Measure integration, Eagle Eye overlay, and the dispatch handoff, all connected.

After that, it's 1% of gross revenue — not a flat monthly fee that stays the same whether you're doing 5 roofs or 60. The platform cost scales with the business. On a slow month, it costs less. On a $4.5M month, it costs $45,000 — and at that volume, $45,000 to keep four states running on one system isn't a cost, it's a rounding error against the model breaking down without it.

Handling the "Why Not Just Use Off-the-Shelf Software?" Objection

Generic CRMs don't know what Eagle Eye overlay is, don't understand the revision-to-roofer-share math, and don't connect Dispatch to a contractor's existing license the way this workflow requires. Off-the-shelf tools mean stitching together 4-5 different apps and hoping they talk to each other across 4 states. This platform was built around the actual sales process — not the other way around.
